Cipherkeep turns an ordinary password into a 256‑bit vault key, then seals your text or files behind authenticated AES‑256‑GCM encryption. Nothing you type is stored, logged, or sent anywhere beyond this one exchange.

 
    Copied ✓

    What happens when you press Encrypt

    01

    Salt & derive

    A random 16‑byte salt is generated and combined with your password through 480,000 rounds of PBKDF2‑HMAC‑SHA256, producing a 256‑bit key.

    02

    Seal with AES‑GCM

    The derived key and a fresh 12‑byte nonce encrypt your data with AES‑256 in Galois/Counter Mode, producing ciphertext plus an authentication tag.

    03

    Package & verify

    Salt, nonce, ciphertext and tag are bundled and Base64‑encoded. On decryption, a wrong password or altered byte makes the tag check fail — loudly, not silently.
